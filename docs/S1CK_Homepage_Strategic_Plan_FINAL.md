# S1CK Pheromone Fragrance: 3D Animated Homepage Strategic Plan

**Prepared for:** S1CK Shop / Jonny Truelove
**Target Conversion Rate:** 4–8%
**Reference Sites Analyzed:** im8health.com · drinkteddytears.com · s1ckshop.com (current audit)
**Date:** April 2026

---

## Executive Summary

The current S1CK Shop homepage (s1ckshop.com) has a strong foundation: a dark luxury aesthetic, a recognizable brand voice, a hero headline with genuine authority ("Rated #1 Pheromones 6 Years In A Row"), and an existing UGC video carousel. However, the page fails to convert at its potential because it relies on repetitive product carousels, low-contrast CTAs, shallow social proof, and a complete absence of immersive storytelling or interactive design. The page is long but not persuasive — each section largely repeats what came before rather than adding a new layer of trust or desire.

To reach a 4–8% conversion rate — placing S1CK in the top tier of DTC fragrance brands, well above the industry median of 2–3% [1] — the new homepage must do two things simultaneously: **convert with precision** and **captivate with experience**. The strategic model is clear. im8health.com, a multi-seven-figure DTC health brand, demonstrates that conversion is built through relentless, layered trust architecture — stacking at least 12 distinct proof elements before the footer, anchoring price through bundles, and reversing risk at every decision point. Drink Teddy Tears demonstrates that a brand can create genuine "wow" through dynamic product presentation, scroll-triggered animation, interactive lifestyle matching, and bold visual storytelling — without requiring heavy WebGL or compromising load speed.

This plan merges both approaches into a single, cohesive homepage architecture across six sections. Every recommendation is grounded in what was observed directly on the reference sites and what the current S1CK site is missing.

## Part I: S1CK Shop Current Site Audit

### What S1CK Does Well

The existing site has several genuine strengths that should be preserved and amplified rather than discarded. The dark black-and-gold color scheme is immediately premium and distinctive, aligning with the brand's positioning as a luxury pheromone fragrance. The headline "Scientifically Formulated to Attract" is punchy and benefit-driven. The "AS SEEN ON" bar with FOX, USA Today, MarketWatch, and Digital Journal provides a credible press foundation. The social proof claim of "Over 100,000+ Happy Customers" with a 5-star rating is a strong asset that is currently underutilized. The existing UGC video carousel — featuring TikTok-style vertical videos with shoppable product overlays — is a genuine competitive advantage that most DTC fragrance brands do not have. The 30-day money-back guarantee and priority shipping trust badges are present. The affiliate section, while minimal, shows that the infrastructure exists.

### What S1CK Must Change

The most critical structural problem is **repetition without progression**. The homepage features at minimum three separate product carousels showing largely the same items, which trains the user to scroll past rather than engage. There is no narrative arc — no escalating reason to buy as the user moves down the page. Each section should add a new dimension of trust, desire, or urgency; currently, most sections simply restate the same proposition.

The second major issue is **CTA weakness**. The primary call-to-action button reads "GET UPTO 50% OFF" in a green-outlined style against a dark background — low contrast, discount-first framing, and a typographical error ("UPTO") that undermines credibility. im8health's primary CTA is "SAVE 30% + GET FREE WELCOME GIFTS →" — the same discount framing, but value-stacked, grammatically precise, and visually dominant.

The third issue is **shallow social proof**. The current site shows star ratings and a customer count, but offers no named testimonials with photos, no influencer endorsements with credentials, no press quotes, no scientific backing, and no awards beyond the "Rated #1" claim. im8health presents 16,255 named reviews, celebrity co-founders, an 8-person scientific advisory board, clinical trial statistics, and athlete endorsements. S1CK's proof layer is a single line where im8health has twelve.

### What S1CK Must Add

The following elements are entirely absent from the current site and represent the highest-leverage additions: a gamified email capture mechanism (replacing the generic "10% off" popup), a named influencer and testimonial section with photos and credentials, a structured subscription tier comparison table, a sensory storytelling layer for each fragrance (since customers cannot smell online), an interactive product discovery module, and a founder narrative section featuring Jonny Truelove's story and mission.

## Part II: Deep-Dive Analysis — im8health.com Conversion Architecture

im8health.com is among the most sophisticated DTC conversion machines currently operating. Every element of the page is engineered to reduce hesitation and amplify desire. The following is a detailed breakdown of the specific mechanisms that drive their conversion rate.

### Above-the-Fold Strategy

The site opens with a sticky announcement bar that stacks three distinct value propositions in a single line: a 30% discount, free welcome gifts, and free shipping on subscriptions. This is not a single offer — it is three simultaneous reasons to act, all visible before the user has read a single word of body copy. The primary CTA in the top-right corner reads "GET STARTED" in a bold, high-contrast red pill button, and it remains sticky throughout the entire scroll journey. The user never loses sight of the path to purchase.

The hero headline — "90 ingredients. 16 supplements replaced. Clinically proven." — is a masterclass in compressed authority. In nine words, it establishes scale (90 ingredients), convenience (16 supplements replaced), and credibility (clinically proven). Below the headline, the aggregate review score of 4.8 stars across 16,255 reviews is displayed immediately, before any product description. This sequencing is deliberate: trust before transaction.

### The 12-Layer Trust Stack

What separates im8health from average DTC sites is not any single trust element but the **accumulation** of proof across the entire page. Before reaching the footer, a visitor encounters the following trust layers in sequence:

**Layer 1 — Celebrity Co-Founder Authority.** David Beckham is not merely an endorser; he is positioned as a co-founder with personal skin in the game. His photo is integrated naturally with the product, and a personal letter from Beckham explains his own health journey. This is aspirational association at its most effective.

**Layer 2 — Aggregate Social Proof.** 16,255 reviews at 4.8 stars, displayed above the fold. The volume makes the rating statistically credible rather than cherry-picked.

**Layer 3 — Clinical Trial Statistics.** Specific percentages from a 12-week clinical trial are cited throughout: "95% reported more energy," "85% better digestion," "80% improved focus." These are not vague claims — they reference a specific San Francisco Research Institute trial with a link to the ClinicalTrials.gov listing.

**Layer 4 — Press Mentions.** A continuous scrolling marquee features logos and quotes from Forbes, NBC Today, Fortune, and Fast Company. The ticker never stops moving, maintaining kinetic energy and reinforcing authority throughout the scroll.

**Layer 5 — Third-Party Certification.** An NSF Certified for Sport badge and third-party testing disclosure appear near every purchase button, addressing the "is this safe and legitimate?" objection.

**Layer 6 — Scientific Advisory Board.** Eight scientists with full names, institutional affiliations (Cedars-Sinai, NASA, Mayo Clinic, Yale), and expandable bios are presented with professional photography. This is institutional credibility that most supplement brands cannot match.

**Layer 7 — Celebrity Athlete Endorsements.** Giannis Antetokounmpo, Aryna Sabalenka, and Ollie Bearman are featured with full-bleed cards, long-form quotes, and the designation "Global Ambassador & Shareholder" — signaling that these individuals have financial stakes in the product's success.

**Layer 8 — Diverse Influencer Testimonials.** Eight additional influencers spanning biohackers, MMA fighters, pharmacists, and nutritionists provide testimonials. The diversity of credential types means different buyer personas each find a relatable authority figure.

**Layer 9 — Competitive Comparison Table.** A side-by-side comparison against an unnamed "leading greens powder" (clearly AG1) shows percentage advantages across key ingredients. The competitor is never named, avoiding legal risk while making the superiority claim undeniable.

**Layer 10 — Social Impact Mission.** A charity partnership section reports 400,000 lives impacted across 65+ countries, with a 99% Charity Navigator rating. This activates purpose-driven buying behavior and creates an emotional dimension beyond the product itself.

**Layer 11 — Risk Reversal.** A 90-day money-back guarantee for subscribers and a 30-day guarantee for monthly customers are placed near every purchase button. The copy reads "Feel it, or get your money back" — confident, simple, and objection-eliminating.

**Layer 12 — Gamified Email Capture.** Rather than a standard discount popup, im8health uses a scratch card mechanic. Users "scratch" a digital card to reveal their discount. This gamification increases engagement, reduces popup dismissal rates, and makes the email capture feel like a reward rather than an interruption.

### Pricing Psychology

The product architecture follows a deliberate decoy pricing strategy. The "Beckham Stack" — a bundle of two products — is presented first and prominently, labeled "BEST VALUE — DAVID'S DAILY RITUAL." Its price of $183 per month is then immediately reframed as "$6.09 USD/day for total body + longevity," reducing the perceived cost through daily amortization. Only after the user has been anchored to the bundle's value are the individual products introduced at lower price points, which now appear modest by comparison. This sequencing consistently drives higher average order values.

## Part III: Deep-Dive Analysis — drinkteddytears.com 3D Experience

Drink Teddy Tears demonstrates that a brand can achieve a genuinely immersive, 3D-feeling web experience without the performance overhead of WebGL or Three.js. Their technical stack — React, CSS animations, Canvas 2D, and autoplay video — is deliberately lean, yet the visual result feels dynamic and alive.

### The Technology Behind the Experience

A technical audit of the site revealed a React-based single-page application with 37 active CSS animations and 368 CSS transition rules. The single canvas element on the page uses a 2D rendering context, not WebGL, meaning all the "3D" effects are achieved through CSS perspective transforms, rotation, and carefully timed keyframe animations. Two autoplay, muted MP4 videos are hosted on Supabase and embedded as background elements. There is no GSAP, no Three.js, no Spline — the entire experience is built on fundamentals executed with exceptional craft.

This is a critical insight for S1CK's development team: **the wow factor does not require expensive or complex technology**. It requires disciplined animation design and a commitment to treating the product as the hero of every scene.

### The Floating Product Hero

The homepage opens with three cans displayed at dynamic, tilted angles against an oversized brand name rendered as background typography. The cans appear to float and hover through a subtle CSS keyframe animation that cycles them through a gentle vertical oscillation. The effect is immediate and visceral — the products feel like physical objects suspended in space rather than flat images on a screen. Decorative lemon slice illustrations are scattered around the cans, reinforcing the flavor identity without any additional copy.

This approach — product as the dominant visual, shown at angles rather than straight-on, with ambient motion — is directly transferable to S1CK's fragrance bottles. A 3D-rendered S1CK bottle shown at a 15–20 degree tilt, slowly rotating and oscillating, would create the same sense of physical presence.

### The Interactive Vibe Picker

The most innovative section on the Teddy Tears site is the "Pick a Vibe. Find Your Match." module. Users are presented with six lifestyle scenario buttons — Rooftop, Backyard, Karaoke, Beach, Picnic, Movie Night — arranged in a scattered, organic layout against a pink background. Clicking any button changes the right-side imagery to show the product in that specific lifestyle context, accompanied by a short evocative description. The Canvas 2D element powers the image transitions.

The conversion logic behind this interaction is sophisticated: it moves the user from passive browsing to active participation, personalizes the product to their lifestyle, and increases time-on-page — all of which are correlated with higher purchase intent. For a fragrance brand, where the sensory experience cannot be replicated digitally, this kind of interactive lifestyle association is especially powerful.

### Color Blocking and Visual Rhythm

Teddy Tears uses bold, contrasting background colors to separate sections and maintain visual momentum. The page moves from light blue (hero) to white (products) to yellow marquee to pink (vibe picker) to dark grey (founder story) to light blue (final CTA). Each color shift signals a new chapter in the brand story and prevents the visual fatigue that comes from a monochromatic scroll. For S1CK's dark-themed site, this principle can be adapted through texture variation, accent color sections, and strategic use of gold or deep crimson to break the all-black monotony.

## Part IV: The Six-Section Homepage Plan

### Section 1 — The Hero Experience: Background Video & Jonny Truelove Intro

**Objective:** Establish the S1CK lifestyle identity, communicate the core value proposition, and drive an immediate first CTA click within the first 5 seconds of the user's visit.

**Current Problem:** The existing hero shows a rotating product video — not a lifestyle video — with Jonny Truelove absent. The CTA is a low-contrast green outline button reading "GET UPTO 50% OFF." The announcement bar promotes a bundle discount but does not stack multiple value propositions. The overall effect is transactional rather than aspirational.

**im8health Conversion Principles Applied:**

The announcement bar must be redesigned to stack three value propositions simultaneously, mirroring im8health's "SAVE 30% + GET FREE WELCOME GIFTS | Free Shipping on All Subscriptions" approach. For S1CK, this could read: **"UNLOCK 15% OFF YOUR FIRST ORDER + FREE SHIPPING | 30-Day Attraction Guarantee."** The bar should be sticky, remaining visible throughout the entire scroll journey.

The primary CTA must be redesigned from the ground up. Replace the green outline button with a solid, high-contrast gold fill button. Replace "GET UPTO 50% OFF" with benefit-forward copy such as **"Discover Your Scent"** or **"Shop the Collection."** The discount can be surfaced as a secondary element (a badge or tooltip) rather than the headline offer, which positions S1CK as a premium brand that happens to offer savings rather than a discount brand.

Immediately below the headline, surface the aggregate social proof: **"★★★★★ Over 100,000+ Happy Customers"** — this asset already exists in S1CK's arsenal and simply needs to be elevated to above-the-fold placement.

The gamified email capture popup should replace the generic "10% off" modal. Consider a "Reveal Your Dominant Scent" interaction where users answer two quick questions (e.g., "What's your vibe?" and "What's your goal?") before their personalized recommendation and discount are revealed. This mirrors im8health's scratch card mechanic — the email capture feels like a reward, not an interruption.

**Teddy Tears 3D Principles Applied:**

The background video must be replaced with a cinematic, lifestyle-forward film featuring Jonny Truelove. The video should show the S1CK lifestyle — confident men and women in aspirational settings — rather than rotating product shots. The product can appear in the video, but the primary subject is the world the product creates.

Overlaid on the video, a high-fidelity 3D rendered S1CK flagship bottle (Le Toxiquè or Liquid Silver) should float in the foreground. Using CSS 3D transforms and a subtle mouse-tracking parallax script, the bottle should respond slightly to the user's cursor position, creating an immediate sense of depth and interactivity. The bottle should oscillate gently — a slow, continuous hover animation — making it feel alive rather than static.

The hero typography should follow Teddy Tears' approach of using the brand name as a visual texture element. The word "S1CK" rendered in large, semi-transparent letters behind the bottle creates depth without additional imagery.

### Section 2 — Social Proof & Authority: Testimonials, Awards, Influencer Mentions

**Objective:** Stack a minimum of eight distinct trust layers in sequence, moving the user from awareness to conviction before they reach the product section.

**Current Problem:** The existing social proof is limited to a logo bar ("AS SEEN ON") and a star rating with customer count. There are no named testimonials with photos, no influencer endorsements with credentials, no scientific backing, and no awards beyond the "Rated #1" claim. This is a single trust layer where the page needs eight or more.

**im8health Conversion Principles Applied:**

The trust architecture for this section should be structured as a deliberate sequence, each layer building on the last. The section opens with a **kinetic press marquee** — a continuous scrolling ticker featuring press logos (FOX, USA Today, MarketWatch, Digital Journal) interspersed with quantitative brand claims: "100,000+ Bottles Sold," "Rated #1 Pheromones 6 Years Running," "30-Day Attraction Guarantee." This mirrors im8health's continuous social proof ticker and maintains the page's kinetic energy.

Below the ticker, introduce a **"Rated #1 Pheromones" award showcase** — a dedicated visual block featuring the House of Pheromones award, any other industry recognitions, and the six-year streak. This is an asset S1CK already possesses and should be treated with the visual weight it deserves: large, centered, with a gold badge treatment.

The most impactful addition is a **named influencer and testimonial grid** modeled directly on im8health's approach. Each card should feature a high-quality photo of the individual, their name, their credential or title (e.g., "Dating Coach," "Men's Lifestyle Influencer," "Verified Buyer"), and a detailed quote — not a snippet, but a full 2–3 sentence testimonial that describes a specific outcome. The diversity of credential types matters: include dating coaches, fitness influencers, men's lifestyle creators, and everyday customers. Each persona speaks to a different buyer archetype.

A **star rating breakdown widget** — showing the distribution of 5, 4, 3, 2, and 1-star reviews — adds statistical credibility. A lopsided distribution heavily weighted toward 5 stars is more convincing than a single aggregate number.

Finally, a **"Why S1CK Works" scientific credibility block** should introduce the pheromone science with accessible language. This does not require a scientific advisory board of the scale im8health employs, but a brief explanation of the mechanism (androstenone, androstenol, or whichever compounds S1CK uses) with a reference to any third-party testing or formulation credentials establishes the brand as evidence-based rather than merely aspirational.

**Teddy Tears 3D Principles Applied:**

The testimonial cards should not be static. As the user scrolls into this section, the cards should animate in from alternating sides — left and right — using CSS scroll-triggered transitions. The marquee ticker should use the same infinite-scroll CSS animation technique Teddy Tears employs for their "SO MANY WAYS TO LAUGH, CRY, & SIP." strip.

The section background should break from the all-black theme. A deep charcoal (#1A1A1A) or textured dark slate background makes the white testimonial cards and gold award badges pop visually, drawing the eye to the human faces and proof elements.

### Section 3 — The 3D Bestsellers Showcase

**Objective:** Present the top three to five S1CK products in a way that communicates luxury, physical presence, and immediate purchase intent — while using pricing psychology to maximize average order value.

**Current Problem:** The existing bestsellers section is a standard Shopify product carousel with flat, straight-on product photography. The same products appear in multiple carousels throughout the page, training users to scroll past rather than engage. There is no visual hierarchy distinguishing the hero product from the supporting products, and no interactive element to deepen engagement.

**im8health Conversion Principles Applied:**

The product architecture must follow im8health's decoy pricing model. The **bundle or flagship product** (e.g., a "The S1CK Arsenal" bundle combining the top two bestsellers) should be presented first, prominently, and labeled with a "Best Value" or "Most Popular" badge. Its price should be displayed in full, then immediately reframed: if the bundle costs $350, the copy should note "That's just $175 per bottle — less than a single department store fragrance." This daily or per-unit cost breakdown reduces price shock and reframes the purchase as rational rather than indulgent.

Below the hero bundle, the top three individual bestsellers — Liquid Silver, Alpha Q, and Le Toxiquè — should be presented as secondary options. Each card must include: the product name, a single-line benefit descriptor (e.g., "The Holy Grail of Attraction"), the price with a strikethrough original price for anchoring, a star rating, and a high-contrast "Add to Cart" button directly on the card. The friction between desire and purchase must be zero — no clicking through to a product page to add to cart.

A **"Why These Work" micro-section** beneath the products — two to three sentences explaining the pheromone science behind the bestsellers — reinforces the scientific credibility established in Section 2 and provides rational justification for an emotionally driven purchase.

**Teddy Tears 3D Principles Applied:**

This is the section where the 3D animation investment delivers its highest return. As the user scrolls into the bestsellers section, the three featured bottles should animate into the viewport using GSAP ScrollTrigger. The recommended animation: each bottle starts slightly below the viewport and slightly rotated, then smoothly rises to its final position while rotating to face the user. The central bottle (the hero product) should be 20–30% larger than the flanking bottles, creating visual hierarchy through scale.

The bottles should be rendered as pre-rendered 3D image sequences rather than live WebGL — a series of 60–120 high-resolution frames showing the bottle at different rotation angles, scrubbed by the user's scroll position via GSAP. This technique, used by Apple for product showcases, delivers photorealistic 3D quality with minimal performance overhead and full mobile compatibility.

On hover, each bottle should respond with a subtle tilt toward the cursor — a CSS 3D transform effect that makes the product feel tangible. The background of this section should feature a very subtle particle effect (small gold dots drifting upward, representing pheromone molecules), implemented as a lightweight Canvas 2D animation.

## Part V: Technical Implementation Roadmap

### Recommended Technology Stack

Achieving the 3D animated experience described in this plan does not require a complete platform migration or a prohibitively expensive development engagement. The following stack is compatible with Shopify's theme architecture and can be implemented as a custom section or headless frontend.

**GSAP (GreenSock Animation Platform) with ScrollTrigger** is the industry standard for scroll-based web animation and should be the primary animation engine for the new homepage. It handles the scroll-triggered bottle reveals, the parallax hero effect, the card entrance animations, and the vibe picker transitions. GSAP is performant, mobile-compatible, and well-documented.

**Pre-rendered 3D Image Sequences** are the recommended approach for the 3D bottle animations. Rather than rendering bottles in real-time via Three.js or WebGL (which introduces significant performance and mobile compatibility risks), the 3D bottles should be rendered offline in Blender or Cinema 4D as a sequence of 120 high-resolution PNG frames showing a full 360-degree rotation. GSAP ScrollTrigger then scrubs through these frames in sync with the user's scroll position, creating a photorealistic 3D effect with the performance profile of a standard image. This is the technique used by Apple for iPhone product pages.

**Canvas 2D** should power the interactive vibe picker transitions, following the same approach used by drinkteddytears.com. The Canvas API provides smooth, hardware-accelerated image transitions without the overhead of a full WebGL context.

**Lottie** (via the Lottie Web player) is recommended for micro-interactions: the guarantee badge animation, the "Add to Cart" success state, and the subscription tier hover effects. Lottie animations are JSON-based, extremely lightweight, and can be designed in Adobe After Effects or Rive.

### Performance Considerations

A 4–8% conversion rate is only achievable if the page loads quickly. Research consistently shows that each additional second of load time reduces conversion rates by 4–7% [2]. The 3D image sequences must be compressed using modern formats (WebP or AVIF) and lazy-loaded below the fold. The background video must be served in compressed MP4 format with a static poster image displayed during loading. All animations should be disabled or simplified on mobile devices where scroll-triggered 3D sequences may cause performance degradation.

| Technology | Use Case | Performance Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| GSAP + ScrollTrigger | All scroll animations, parallax, reveals | Minimal — ~30KB library |
| Pre-rendered Image Sequences | 3D bottle rotation on scroll | Medium — optimize with WebP, lazy load |
| Canvas 2D | Vibe picker transitions | Minimal — no WebGL overhead |
| Lottie | Micro-interactions, badges | Minimal — JSON-based |
| Autoplay Video | Hero background, founder section | Medium — compress to <5MB, serve via CDN |
